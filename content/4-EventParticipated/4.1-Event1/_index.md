# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day”

### Event Objectives

- Learn how to become "addicted to learning like you're addicted to social media."
- Introduce Automated Prompt Engineering techniques.
- Develop skills and the right mindset for the AI era.
- Learn about a software project management methodology optimized for AI-assisted development.

### Speakers

- **Huynh Hoang Long** – Admin of FCAJ
- **Nguyen Tuan Thinh** – DevOps/Cloud Engineer, First Cloud AI Journey
- **Khang** – Solution Architect
- **Thao** – Software Developer

### Key Highlights

### Addicted to Learning Like You're Addicted to Social Media

- Why does our brain prefer social media over studying?
- Turning knowledge into a dopamine-driven reward.
- Three ways to hack your brain to make learning feel like playing.

### Automated Prompt Engineering: Enhancing LLM Output Quality

- Why is Prompt Engineering important?
- What makes an effective prompt?
- Understanding tokens, AI costs, and advanced prompting techniques.
- Live demonstration.

### AI-Ready Freshers: Skills and Mindset in the AI Era

- How has AI changed the way we work?
- Will AI replace humans?
- The importance of learning from mistakes and maintaining integrity.
- Five benefits of working in the industry.
- How companies evaluate candidates.

### BMAD Method

- How humans can collaborate effectively with AI.
- Assigning AI specialized roles such as Product Manager (PM), Architect, Developer, and Scrum Master.
- Breaking large projects into smaller Epics and User Stories instead of asking AI to complete the entire project at once.

### Key Takeaways

### Effective Learning Strategies

- Understood why our brains are naturally more attracted to social media than studying and learned how to leverage dopamine mechanisms to maintain learning motivation.
- Learned how to break large learning goals into smaller, manageable tasks.

### Prompt Engineering

- Learned the essential components of an effective prompt.
- Understood how tokens affect AI usage costs.
- Explored advanced prompting techniques that improve the accuracy and quality of AI responses.

### Working in the AI Era

- Realized that AI is a powerful productivity tool rather than a replacement for humans.
- Learned the qualities and skills that employers value in candidates.

### BMAD Method

- Learned how to assign AI specific professional roles.
- Understood how to decompose a large project into smaller Epics and User Stories for more efficient development.

### Applying to Work

- Applied Prompt Engineering techniques when using AI tools such as ChatGPT and Gemini.
- Adopted the learning strategies introduced during the event to maintain long-term motivation and improve learning efficiency.

### Event Experience

Attending the **FCAJ Community Day** was extremely valuable, giving me a comprehensive view of modernizing applications and databases using advanced methods and tools. Key experiences included:

### Learning from Experienced Speakers

- The speakers shared valuable real-world experiences about learning, career development, and applying AI in professional environments.
- I gained a better understanding of how to build a long-term learning mindset instead of focusing solely on memorizing knowledge.

### Hands-on Technical Knowledge

- Learned how to design effective prompts to maximize the capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs).

### Exploring Modern Development Practices

- Learned about the **BMAD** methodology for collaborating with AI during software development.
- Understood how to break a large project into smaller Epics and User Stories before assigning specific tasks to AI according to different professional roles.

### Networking and Discussion

- Through conversations with other participants, I gained new perspectives on effective learning, practical AI adoption, and personal career development in the AI era.

#### Lessons learned

- To make the most of AI, it is essential to ask clear questions and provide well-defined requirements.
- Breaking complex problems into smaller tasks and assigning appropriate roles to AI significantly improves the quality of the results.
- Building consistent learning habits is more effective than relying solely on temporary motivation.
- Although AI is an extremely powerful assistant, humans remain responsible for reviewing, validating, and taking ownership of the final results.

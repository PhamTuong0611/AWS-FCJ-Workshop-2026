Event 2

Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day”

Event Objectives

  • Share insights into the current job market.
  • Learn how to use AI effectively and provide AI with the correct context.
  • Share experiences from participating in hackathons.
  • Introduce AI tools that support the software development lifecycle.

Speakers

  • Nguyen Gia Hung - Head of Solution Architect
  • Tinh Truong - Platform Engineer, GoTymeX
  • Hai Anh - AWS Community Builder
  • Nguyen Tuan Thinh - DevOps Engineer, First Cloud AI Journey
  • Team VIB:Mai, Thao, Uyen - GenAI Engineer, VIB
  • Dao Duc - Solution Architect, Cloud Kinetics
  • Cat Vy - Senior Business Systems Analyst, VPBank

Key Highlights

Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

  • Why AI fails without sufficient context and what “context” really means.
  • The evolution of AI from prompts to memory (the concept of the “Second AI Brain”).
  • How providing better context leads to better AI outputs.
  • Career guidance on how students can start building AI-related skills.
  • Q&A session.

Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

  • Quick Chat: An AI assistant for data exploration and insight generation.
  • Quick Flow: Build intelligent workflows using natural language without coding.
  • Quick Space: A collaborative workspace for sharing insights and knowledge.
  • Quick Sight: Create dashboards and reports from raw data using natural language.

From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

  • Amazon CloudFront for various workloads.
  • Cost optimization with Amazon CloudFront.
  • Security capabilities.
  • Improving system reliability.
  • Performance optimization using CloudFront.

36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

  • Why the team joined LotusHacks.
  • Brainstorming ideas from scratch.
  • Defining the problem and shaping UTMorpho.
  • Building under pressure during a 36-hour development sprint.
  • Challenges faced during the competition.
  • UTMorpho product overview and live demo.
  • Lessons learned and future plans.

Non-Determinism of “Deterministic” LLM Settings

  • How LLMs select the next token.
  • Why inference optimizations can produce unexpected behavior.
  • Practical impacts.
  • Risk mitigation strategies.

​Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring

  • Structural incompatibilities between banking systems and startup data.
  • When to use Single-Agent AI and when not to.
  • Multi-Agent AI architecture.
  • Safety and compliance considerations.
  • Q&A session.

Key Takeaways

Effective AI Usage

  • Learned that context is the key factor determining the quality of AI responses.
  • Understood how to provide sufficient context so AI can better understand requests and generate more accurate results.
  • Gained better direction on how students can develop AI skills for both learning and future careers.

AI in Data Analytics and Application Development

  • Explored Amazon Q and its capabilities for data exploration, business insights, and dashboard creation using natural language.
  • Learned how AI can automate workflows without requiring complex programming.

Project Development and AI

  • Understood that time management, teamwork, and adaptability under pressure are critical for successful project delivery.
  • Learned about the Multi-Agent AI approach, how multiple AI agents collaborate to solve complex problems, and the importance of safety and compliance in enterprise AI systems.

Applying to Work

  • Applied context-building techniques when using ChatGPT, Gemini, and Amazon Q to improve AI understanding and response quality.
  • Learned product development strategies from hackathon experiences to strengthen teamwork, time management, and problem-solving skills under pressure.

Event Experience

Attending the FCAJ Community Day was extremely valuable, giving me a comprehensive view of modernizing applications and databases using advanced methods and tools. Key experiences included:

Learning from Industry Experts

  • The speakers shared valuable real-world experiences about AI trends, the technology job market, and essential skills for the industry.
  • I gained a clearer understanding of how to prepare myself for the rapid evolution of AI.
  • Insights from AWS experts and industry professionals also helped me shape my career goals and learning roadmap.

Hands-on Technical Knowledge

  • Learned how to build effective context so AI can better understand user requirements and produce higher-quality responses.
  • Explored the concept of the Second AI Brain, the evolution of AI from prompts to memory, and techniques for working more effectively with AI.
  • Gained additional knowledge about Amazon CloudFront, system performance optimization, and the non-deterministic behavior of Large Language Models (LLMs).

Networking and Knowledge Sharing

  • Explored Amazon Quick as an AI-powered solution for data analytics, dashboard creation, and workflow automation using natural language.
  • Learned about the Multi-Agent AI approach and how multiple AI agents collaborate to solve complex enterprise problems.
  • Observed real-world applications of AI in finance, data analytics, and product development.

Lessons learned

  • The event provided an excellent opportunity to interact with speakers, engineers, and fellow students who share a passion for AI and AWS.
